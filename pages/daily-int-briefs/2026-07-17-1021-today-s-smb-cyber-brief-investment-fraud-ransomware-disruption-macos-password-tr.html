Today’s SMB cyber brief: investment fraud, ransomware disruption, macOS password traps, and Fortinet urgency

What small and medium-sized businesses should look out for today.

High Friday 17 July 2026, 10:21 UK time
Today’s look-out: Fraud and ransomware disruption, plus urgent supplier/edge-device exposure

What to look out for today

  • Investment fraud and money laundering: continued large-scale “investment/crypto” scam activity, with criminals moving stolen funds through laundering networks.
  • Ransomware causing real operational disruption: a major brand has publicly reported ransomware halting production at a subsidiary, a reminder that cyber incidents can quickly become supply and fulfilment issues.
  • macOS password-stealing pressure tactics: a macOS stealer reported to force users into entering their login password.
  • Fortinet urgency (actively exploited): CISA is flagging actively exploited flaws affecting Fortinet FortiSandbox. If you or your IT provider runs it, treat as urgent.

Why this matters to smaller businesses

  • Finance teams are a prime target: investment fraud lures and “quick profit” pitches often reach owners, bookkeepers and payroll/finance staff.
  • Disruption spreads through suppliers: if a larger supplier is hit by ransomware, SMEs can see knock-on impacts (delays, shortages, missed SLAs, rushed workarounds).
  • mac users are not immune: if you have directors or staff on MacBooks, credential theft can lead to email account takeover, invoice fraud, and data access.
  • Your MSP may be the one running exposed kit: even if you don’t manage security appliances yourself, your outsourced IT provider might.

Warning signs

  • Unexpected calls/messages pitching “guaranteed returns”, crypto investments, or asking you to move funds “to secure accounts”.
  • Urgent requests to change payee details, especially if the request arrives via email, WhatsApp, or a new contact address.
  • Staff reporting a Mac behaving oddly (apps closing, being blocked from using the computer normally) followed by prompts to enter a password to “fix” something.
  • Sudden supplier updates about delayed shipments, system outages, or manual processing—prime time for criminals to impersonate that supplier.

How attackers may exploit the situation

  • Fraud rings monetise stolen funds fast: laundering activity enables more scams and makes recovery harder once money is sent.
  • Ransomware news creates cover: criminals impersonate affected brands/suppliers, sending fake “updated bank details”, “re-issue payment” or “download new invoice” emails.
  • Credential theft leads to invoice fraud: once an email account is compromised (especially finance mailboxes), attackers watch conversations and insert altered payment instructions.
  • Exploited security appliances increase blast radius: if perimeter/security tooling is compromised, attackers may gain access to internal systems without obvious early warning.

What to do today

  • Re-brief staff (2 minutes): no investment pitches, no “new bank details” accepted by email—verify using a known phone number from your records.
  • Add a payment-change control: require a second-person approval + call-back for any change of bank details or payment destination.
  • macOS users: remind staff to stop and call IT if their Mac suddenly forces unusual password prompts or behaves abnormally.
  • Supplier spoofing defence: if a key supplier reports disruption, expect impersonation attempts—tighten checks on invoices, delivery changes, and account details.

Ask your IT provider

  • Do we (or you) run Fortinet FortiSandbox anywhere in our environment or as part of your managed service? If yes, what is the mitigation/status today?
  • What monitoring is in place for business email compromise and suspicious mailbox rules/forwarding (common after credential theft)?
  • What is our rapid response process if a director/finance mailbox is suspected compromised (containment steps, password resets, session revocation)?
  • Can you confirm our backup and recovery approach is tested and that ransomware response contacts are up to date?

Patch watch - only one short paragraph, and only if relevant

CISA is warning about actively exploited vulnerabilities in Fortinet FortiSandbox. This is most relevant if your organisation (or your MSP) uses FortiSandbox; treat this as an urgent supplier/edge-device risk and ask for same-day confirmation of exposure and remediation plans.

One action today

Send a short internal note today: “No payment detail changes by email—must be verified by call-back to a known number and approved by a second person.”
