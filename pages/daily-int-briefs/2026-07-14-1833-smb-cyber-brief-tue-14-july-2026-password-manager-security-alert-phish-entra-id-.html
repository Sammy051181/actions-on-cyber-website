SMB Cyber Brief (Tue 14 July 2026): Password manager ‘security alert’ phish + Entra ID passkeys change to prep for

What small and medium-sized businesses should look out for today.

High Tuesday 14 July 2026, 18:33 UK time
Today’s look-out: Credential theft scams (fake security alerts) and identity/login disruption risk

What to look out for today

  • Phishing emails/texts pretending to be “security alerts” from password managers (notably LastPass and Bitwarden), pushing staff to click through to fake sign-in pages.
  • Identity and login change risk: Microsoft says passkeys will become the default sign-in method for Entra ID from September 2026 — organisations using Microsoft 365/Entra should plan user comms and support.
  • Operational disruption is still a common outcome: a major taxi operator shut down parts of its infrastructure after a cyberattack, showing how quickly day-to-day services can be impacted.

Why this matters to smaller businesses

Password managers often protect the “keys to the kingdom” (email, payroll, banking, Microsoft 365, remote access). If one staff member types their master password into a fake page, attackers may be able to take over multiple business systems quickly.

Separately, changes to sign-in defaults (like passkeys in Entra ID) can cause confusion, lockouts, or insecure workarounds if users aren’t prepared — especially in small teams without dedicated IT on hand.

Warning signs

  • Messages claiming: “Unusual sign-in”, “Vault access attempt”, “Action required”, “Your account will be locked”, or “Reset now”.
  • Links that go to a sign-in page that doesn’t look quite right, or asks for the master password unexpectedly.
  • Staff reporting sudden MFA prompts they didn’t initiate, or being asked to “approve” a login.
  • Unexpected password reset emails for Microsoft 365, password manager accounts, or key admin accounts.

How attackers may exploit the situation

  • Credential harvesting: fake “security alert” pages capture master passwords (and sometimes MFA codes) for password managers.
  • Account takeover chaining: once inside a vault, attackers try the stored credentials against email, finance systems, cloud consoles, and remote access.
  • Business email compromise and payment fraud: access to email and saved logins can enable invoice redirection, supplier bank detail change scams, and fake payroll requests.
  • Disruption pressure: intruders may move quickly to lock accounts or disrupt systems to force urgent decision-making (and sometimes pave the way for ransomware).

What to do today

  • Send a 2-line staff warning: “We’re seeing fake ‘password manager security alert’ messages. Don’t click links. If worried, open the app directly and report the message to IT.”
  • Verify password manager hygiene: ensure MFA is on for all password manager accounts, especially admins; confirm recovery options are up to date.
  • Put a calendar hold for Entra ID passkeys planning: identify who owns user comms, support, and policy decisions ahead of September.
  • Protect finance workflows: re-brief the team that supplier bank changes and urgent payment requests must be verified out-of-band (known phone number, not email reply).

Ask your IT provider

  • Can you confirm which users have LastPass/Bitwarden (or other password managers) and whether MFA is enforced for all of them?
  • Do we have alerting for suspicious sign-ins to Microsoft 365/Entra ID and our password manager admin console?
  • What is our plan for Entra ID passkeys becoming default in September 2026 — who will be piloted first, and how will we support staff who struggle?
  • If a password manager account is compromised, what’s our rapid response playbook (reset priorities, session revocation, admin account checks, finance controls)?

Patch watch - only one short paragraph, and only if relevant

Today’s main risks are phishing and identity/process issues rather than a specific patch rush. If you run industrial/critical manufacturing kit, note that ABB issued an advisory for T-MAC Plus; ensure your operational tech supplier or maintainer is tracking vendor updates and has a safe maintenance plan.

One action today

Send a short internal alert telling staff to ignore “password manager security alert” links and to only sign in via the app/bookmark (then report the message).
