Weekend lookout: urgent action for ShareFile on‑prem connectors and risky crafted emails targeting webmail

What small and medium-sized businesses should look out for today.

High Saturday 11 July 2026, 09:58 UK time
Today’s look-out: Supplier/SaaS dependency risk and email-led compromise

What to look out for today

Two practical weekend risks to sanity-check if you run IT systems in-house or via an outsourced provider:

  • Progress ShareFile (Storage Zone Controllers): Progress is warning customers using on-premises Storage Zone Controllers to shut down servers due to a “credible external security threat”.
  • Zimbra Classic Web Client: Zimbra has warned of a crafted email issue that could allow malicious code to run in a user’s webmail session (i.e. when someone opens/reads a message in the web interface).

Why this matters to smaller businesses

  • File-sharing and email are high-value choke points. If either is compromised, attackers can steal data, reset passwords, trick staff into paying invoices, or move on to ransomware.
  • SMEs often depend on a small number of key systems (ShareFile/Zimbra/hosted IT). Disruption on a weekend can quickly become a Monday business outage.
  • These risks can look like normal work. A user opens a message, clicks a link, or uploads a file as usual—until accounts start sending fraud emails or files go missing.

Warning signs

  • Unexpected prompts to re-authenticate to webmail or file-sharing, especially after opening an email.
  • Emails that appear to come from internal colleagues/partners but contain odd formatting, unexpected attachments, or “view document” links.
  • New or unfamiliar forwarding rules, mailbox delegates, or “sent items” you don’t recognise.
  • Users reporting that shared files/folders have changed permissions or links were created without their knowledge.
  • Unusual login alerts (new locations/devices), or multiple staff reporting account lockouts.

How attackers may exploit the situation

  • Email-led session attacks: a crafted email can cause malicious behaviour inside a user’s webmail session, potentially leading to credential theft or actions taken “as the user”.
  • Supplier targeting: attackers focus on widely used business platforms (secure file sharing, email) because one entry point can provide access to many organisations.
  • Follow-on fraud and ransomware: once inside email or file-sharing, attackers may pivot to invoice fraud (changing bank details), data theft, and disruptive encryption.

What to do today

  • Find out if you use ShareFile Storage Zone Controllers. If yes, treat this as urgent: confirm with your IT provider what is being done today (including whether systems should be taken offline as advised by the supplier).
  • Confirm whether you run Zimbra (especially the Classic Web Client). If yes, ensure the business knows to be extra cautious with unexpected emails and links until your provider confirms mitigations/updates are in place.
  • Send a short staff note (2 minutes): “Be cautious of unexpected emails/attachments this weekend. If a message looks odd, don’t click links—report it.”
  • Double-check payment change controls: make sure bank-detail changes still require a call-back to a known number (not a number in the email).
  • Make sure you can recover: verify you have recent offline/immutable backups and that you know who to call if accounts get taken over.

Ask your IT provider

  • Do we run Progress ShareFile Storage Zone Controllers anywhere? If yes, what’s the immediate action plan and who is monitoring it over the weekend?
  • Do we run Zimbra Classic Web Client? What protections are in place to reduce email-borne session risks?
  • What logs/alerts are we actively monitoring for mass mailbox rule changes, suspicious logins, and unusual file-sharing activity?
  • If we need to take a service offline, what is the business continuity plan (temporary alternatives for file transfer and email access)?
  • What’s our agreed incident response process for email account takeover and ransomware (contacts, priorities, and decision-making)?

Patch watch - only one short paragraph, and only if relevant

If you use Zimbra or ShareFile components on your own servers (or your MSP hosts them for you), treat supplier security advisories as urgent operational items: confirm today what has been updated, what has been temporarily disabled (if required), and what extra monitoring is in place until normal service resumes.

One action today

Message your IT provider today to confirm whether you use ShareFile Storage Zone Controllers or Zimbra Classic Web Client, and get a same-day plan for mitigation/monitoring (including taking ShareFile on-prem components offline if applicable).
