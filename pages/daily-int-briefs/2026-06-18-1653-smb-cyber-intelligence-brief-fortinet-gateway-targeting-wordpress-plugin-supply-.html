SMB Cyber Intelligence Brief — Fortinet gateway targeting, WordPress plugin supply-chain risk, and Salesforce/OAuth data theft

What small and medium-sized businesses should look out for today.

High Thursday 18 June 2026, 16:53 UK time
Today’s look-out: Supplier/SaaS compromise and remote access gateway targeting

What to look out for today

Three themes SMEs should treat as live risks today:

  • Remote access gateways under pressure: UK NCSC has issued advice after global targeting of Fortinet firewalls and VPN gateways. If you use Fortinet for remote access or site-to-site VPN, assume criminals are actively hunting for easy entry points.
  • Website supply-chain compromise: A vendor’s official update system for multiple ShapedPlugin WordPress plugins was reportedly compromised, pushing infected releases to paying customers. If your website relies on WordPress plugins, this is a “trust the update” problem.
  • SaaS login tokens abused to steal data: A reported OAuth breach at Klue is linked to “Icarus” activity where criminals steal Salesforce CRM data and attempt extortion. This is a reminder that third-party app connections to core systems can become a backdoor.

Why this matters to smaller businesses

  • Single points of failure: One compromised firewall/VPN can expose email, file shares, remote desktop and cloud admin accounts.
  • Websites are business-critical: A poisoned plugin update can lead to site defacement, SEO spam, customer redirection, data theft, or reputational damage.
  • CRMs contain your crown jewels: Salesforce often holds customer lists, deal pipelines, contracts and notes—exactly what extortion groups want.
  • Dependency risk: These incidents don’t require your staff to “click a bad link” to hurt you; they can arrive via suppliers, tools, or trusted integrations.

Warning signs

  • Fortinet / remote access: unexpected VPN logins, new user accounts, configuration changes you didn’t request, unusual admin logins outside normal hours, staff reporting they can’t connect or MFA prompts behaving oddly.
  • WordPress: sudden redirects, new admin users, plugins you don’t recognise, unexplained changes to site files/themes, traffic spikes, customers reporting odd pop-ups, checkout pages behaving differently.
  • Salesforce / OAuth: new “Connected App” approvals you don’t recognise, users seeing repeated consent prompts, access from unusual locations, bulk exports, unusual API activity, unexpected password/MFA reset emails.
  • Scam follow-on: emails/calls claiming to be your “IT support”, “hosting company”, “Fortinet partner”, “WordPress vendor” or “Salesforce security team” urging urgent action, payment, or remote access.

How attackers may exploit the situation

  • Gateway entry → ransomware: criminals commonly use exposed or poorly managed remote access devices as an initial foothold, then move across the network for data theft and disruption.
  • Trusted updates: a compromised plugin update channel can install unwanted code on websites without anyone downloading something “dodgy”.
  • OAuth token abuse: attackers may not need a password if they can obtain/abuse OAuth access granted to a third-party app—allowing silent access to CRM data and exports.
  • Extortion pressure: stolen CRM or customer data may be used to pressure payment, including threats to contact customers or publish data.

What to do today

  • Confirm whether you use Fortinet: if yes, ask your IT provider to confirm what actions they’ve taken in response to the NCSC advice and what monitoring is in place for admin/VPN activity.
  • Check your WordPress plugin inventory: identify whether any ShapedPlugin plugins are installed (including on older/forgotten sites). If you outsource web support, ask for written confirmation and what was checked.
  • Review SaaS integrations: in Salesforce (and other key SaaS platforms), review connected apps/integrations and remove anything not clearly required. Tighten who is allowed to approve new app connections.
  • Brief staff (10 minutes): warn about “urgent security” calls/emails pretending to be firewall/hosting/CRM support and pushing remote access or payment.
  • Backups and recovery: make sure you can restore website content and critical data quickly (including Microsoft 365 and SaaS data) and that restores have been tested.

Ask your IT provider

  • Do we have any Fortinet firewalls/VPN gateways? Which sites, and who administers them?
  • What logs are you reviewing for VPN/admin access, and how quickly would you spot suspicious access?
  • Do we have an asset list of all websites and WordPress plugins we run (including microsites and old campaigns)?
  • How do we handle plugin updates safely (e.g., staging/testing, integrity checks, monitoring for unexpected changes)?
  • For Salesforce and other SaaS, who can approve OAuth/Connected Apps, and how often are integrations reviewed?
  • If an extortion email claims they have our CRM data, what is our first-hour plan (evidence capture, access revocation, customer comms, legal/insurance)?

Patch watch - only one short paragraph, and only if relevant

The UK NCSC alert on Fortinet gateway targeting is a strong prompt to ensure your remote access devices are being actively managed (including timely vendor updates and secure configuration). Treat any internet-facing firewall/VPN as a priority business risk, not a “set and forget” device.

One action today

Today, confirm with your IT/web provider whether you use Fortinet gateways or ShapedPlugin WordPress plugins, and request a same-day check of VPN/admin logs plus a review of WordPress plugins and Salesforce connected apps for anything unexpected.
