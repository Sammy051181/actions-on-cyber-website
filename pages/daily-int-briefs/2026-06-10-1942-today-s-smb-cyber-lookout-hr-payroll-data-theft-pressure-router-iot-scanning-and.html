Daily SMB Cyber Intelligence Brief

Today’s SMB cyber lookout: HR/payroll data theft pressure, router/IoT scanning, and ‘AI email assistant’ phishing risk

What small and medium-sized businesses should look out for today.

High Wednesday 10 June 2026, 19:42 UK time
Today’s look-out: Supplier/SaaS data theft & extortion pressure plus SOHO/IoT exposure scanning

What to look out for today

1) HR/payroll & finance data theft/extortion themes: reports of ongoing data-theft attacks targeting Oracle PeopleSoft environments by the ShinyHunters extortion group. If you run PeopleSoft (or a hosted PeopleSoft service), assume increased risk of data exposure and follow-on scams.

2) Internet scanning of small-office devices: reports that the JDY botnet (made up of SOHO and IoT devices) is expanding and is being used to discover and map exposed services. That usually translates into more background “knocking” on your firewall, routers, NAS, cameras, remote admin ports and cloud gateways.

3) AI email/agent safety: research showing an AI email agent can fall for phishing and spill data. If your business is trialling AI agents that read email or handle tickets, treat them like a high-privilege user that can be tricked.

Why this matters to smaller businesses

  • Payroll/HR systems hold high-value data (NI numbers, bank details, addresses, salary info). When attackers steal it, SMEs often face extortion demands and then secondary fraud (impersonation, fake invoice/payment change, payroll diversion).
  • Most SMEs rely on routers, firewalls and cloud portals that are frequently scanned. If something is exposed to the internet “by accident”, it can be found quickly.
  • AI tools can amplify mistakes: an assistant that auto-reads, summarises, or forwards messages can unintentionally leak sensitive content or help an attacker move faster.

Warning signs

  • Unexpected HR/payroll emails referencing “data breach”, “urgent verification”, “employee tax documents”, or “secure portal login”, especially if they push a link or attachment.
  • Supplier/IT messages claiming urgent action for PeopleSoft, Oracle, or “HR database security incident”, asking you to reset credentials or share exports.
  • New MFA prompts you didn’t initiate, or password reset emails you didn’t request (especially for HR, finance and admin accounts).
  • Spikes in firewall/ISP alerts, unusual remote login attempts, or device management pages being accessed unexpectedly.
  • AI agent behaviour changes: auto-forwarding, creating unusual drafts, sharing content to external recipients, or generating “helpful” summaries that include confidential data.

How attackers may exploit the situation

  • Extortion and follow-on scams: after a theft, criminals often pivot to impersonating IT/HR, sending “employee data verification” or “bank details update” requests, and targeting finance with payment redirection.
  • Scanning → intrusion: botnets that map exposed services help attackers identify businesses with remote admin open, weak credentials, or misconfigured gateways.
  • Phishing via automation: if an AI agent can be tricked into opening or acting on a message, it may summarise sensitive content, exfiltrate data, or escalate a malicious request into a ticket/workflow that looks legitimate.

What to do today

  • Brief HR & Finance (10 minutes): treat any breach-themed email as suspicious; verify bank detail changes by calling a known number (not one in the email).
  • Check what’s internet-exposed: confirm your router/firewall/NAS/cameras/remote admin aren’t exposing management interfaces to the internet unless absolutely necessary.
  • Lock down HR/payroll access: ensure MFA is enabled for admin accounts; reduce who can export employee lists and bank details.
  • If you use AI agents on email/tickets: review permissions (least privilege), turn off auto-forwarding/auto-actions where possible, and prevent the agent from accessing payroll/finance mailboxes unless essential.
  • Be ready for comms: decide who in your business handles suspected data exposure and how you will contact your IT provider quickly.

Ask your IT provider

  • Do we run Oracle PeopleSoft anywhere (on-prem or hosted)? If yes, what extra monitoring is in place for unusual access or data export activity?
  • Can you confirm no device management interfaces (firewall/router/NAS/cameras/RDP-like access) are exposed to the internet without strong controls?
  • What alerts are we set to receive for impossible travel, repeated login failures, and MFA fatigue attempts on admin/finance/HR accounts?
  • If we’re trialling AI email/ticket agents, what data can they access, where is it stored, and can we restrict or audit their actions?

Patch watch - only one short paragraph, and only if relevant

Several major vendors are issuing fixes this week (including SAP, Ivanti and Fortinet). For most SMEs this is relevant mainly if you (or your MSP) run these products directly. Ask your IT provider to confirm whether any of your internet-facing gateways or business-critical platforms fall into this category and what their change window is for applying updates.

One action today

Send a short internal note to HR and Finance today: “No bank detail changes or payroll updates by email—verify via a known phone number; report any breach-themed ‘urgent verification’ messages immediately.”
