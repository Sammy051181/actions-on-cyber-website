SMB Cyber Daily Brief (Tue 09 Jun): VPN ransomware entry, WhatsApp spear-phishing, and Chrome exploit activity

What small and medium-sized businesses should look out for today.

High Tuesday 09 June 2026, 11:42 UK time
Today’s look-out: Remote access compromise + high-quality phishing (WhatsApp/email) leading to ransomware and account takeover

What to look out for today

  • Remote access/VPN risk: ransomware affiliates are reportedly exploiting a Check Point remote access/VPN issue in zero-day attacks. If your business (or your IT provider) uses Check Point for remote access, treat this as urgent.
  • WhatsApp spear-phishing: targeted messages trying to trick recipients into clicking links that lead to external sites (linked to spyware-style campaigns). This is more “carefully targeted” than typical spam.
  • Browser compromise risk: Google has issued emergency updates for a Chrome zero-day being exploited “in the wild”. If staff browse the web on work devices, this matters even without them installing anything.
  • Android banking/payment lure: fake “banking app updates” being distributed via GitHub. A common theme is pushing users to install an update from outside official app stores.
  • Third-party data exposure: a financial firm’s Hong Kong subsidiary reported a breach tied to a third-party vendor database—another reminder that supplier incidents can trigger convincing follow-on scams.

Why this matters to smaller businesses

SMEs are often hit through the fastest paths to disruption: remote access systems (VPNs), stolen logins via convincing messages, or staff devices being compromised through routine browsing. Any of these can lead to mailbox takeover, invoice fraud, data theft, or ransomware downtime. Supplier incidents can also spark realistic “we’ve changed our bank details” or “verify your account” scams that target finance and admin teams.

Warning signs

  • Unexpected password reset/MFA prompts or staff reporting they’ve been “logged out” of email/365/Google accounts.
  • WhatsApp messages (or texts) that create urgency and push a link: “policy breach”, “account complaint”, “verify now”, “you’ve been reported”, “missed delivery”, “invoice attached”.
  • Users asked to install an app/update from a link (especially Android) or “update your banking app” outside the official store process.
  • Unusual remote access behaviour: staff can’t connect, new devices appear, logins at odd hours, spikes in failed sign-ins.
  • Finance team receives a sudden bank detail change request or new payment instructions following any “supplier breach” news.

How attackers may exploit the situation

  • VPN/remote access compromise to gain an initial foothold, then steal credentials, move into file shares and backups, and trigger ransomware disruption.
  • WhatsApp spear-phishing to harvest credentials or lure a user onto a malicious site, then pivot into email, finance approvals, or customer data.
  • Chrome exploit activity to compromise a device simply through browsing, then use that access to steal saved sessions/cookies or drop further malware.
  • Fake “banking app update” flows to capture payment credentials or enable payment interception on mobile devices.

What to do today

  • Message staff (2 minutes): remind everyone not to click WhatsApp/SMS links for “account verification” and to report anything that feels targeted.
  • Lock down remote access: confirm who uses VPN, ensure MFA is enforced, and temporarily tighten access (geo/role restrictions) where practical.
  • Update browsers promptly: ensure Chrome updates are applied across company devices (including shared/front-desk PCs).
  • Mobile hygiene: tell staff not to install “banking updates” from links and to use official app stores only.
  • Finance control check: re-brief on call-back verification for any bank detail change or urgent payment request.

Ask your IT provider

  • Do we (or any of our suppliers/MSP tools) use Check Point Remote Access/VPN anywhere? If yes, what immediate mitigations and monitoring have you applied?
  • Are we enforcing MFA on all remote access and email, and do we block “legacy”/weaker sign-in methods?
  • How quickly do you roll out emergency browser updates (e.g., Chrome) across managed devices, including laptops not currently in the office?
  • What logs/alerts are you actively watching for VPN anomalies and mass login failures?
  • What is our ransomware disruption plan (restore time expectations, backup isolation, and who does what on day one)?

Patch watch - only one short paragraph, and only if relevant

Two patch themes to prioritise today: (1) emergency Chrome updates due to active exploitation, and (2) urgent attention to Check Point remote access/VPN deployments because ransomware groups are reportedly exploiting a zero-day. If you outsource IT, ask for confirmation of completion and what monitoring is in place afterwards.

One action today

Send a same-day staff note: “No clicking WhatsApp/SMS ‘verify your account’ links; report suspicious messages; install updates only from official sources,” and remind finance to call-verify any bank detail changes.
